The Curious Case of Depression

We start off with a map illustrating the number of depression cases per country. From first sight it is apparent, that almost all countries have depression cases in the millions. The notable countries that are worst hit are Ukraine and Congo both with cases above 2 million after normalised for population. A bleak picture indeed !!

üìâ What are the common themes? üìâ

Academic strain is nothing new. The radial chart shows undergraduates and fresh graduates taking the brunt of depression in the academic world. This is insightful since it correlates with undergraduates needing to handle deadlines and exam stress. On the flip side, fresh graduates struggle to find dignified jobs.

Regular internet access is the main culprit in most depression cases. This is not surprising since the modern human spends an immense volume of time on social media platforms which are often attributed to anxiety, mood swings, lack of concentration, tiredness and obsessive thinking all of which follow suit as shown above.

üßê Curious Insights üßê

This chart shows no correlation between expenses and children which is often cited as a reason for high cost in mainstream media. Individuals with zero or more are children are spread on the expenses column signifying no solid relation between the two. Rather a significant number of depression cases are concentrated at individuals with low value assets signified by the high density of lines in the lowest asset region.

This chart tells a story from a different angle. Even if men are more depressed in general as seen on the left chart, women tend to have a higher suicide count. Another insight is that young and senior adults make up for most of the cases. This is alarming since it shows the current and future workforce don't possess a healthy mind - a crucial element to keep the economic wheel spinning.

üîÜ Peek Into the Bright Side üîÜ

All is not lost!! We look at the top 5 most happiest nations on the planet and filter some common traits. First off is gdp per capita, which is high among all the 5 nations, indicating that a significant portion of the population must contribute to the economy for the greater good.


At second place comes social support, that is body of governance on which the population can rely on.


Next up is life expectancy and freedom of life choices. It is no surprise that if a population can live long and healthy, they have the opportunity to do explore and do more in life.


The first three may not be possible for all countries, but that's not all. Next up is generosity. This does not require any money or resources - all it takes is kindness and the impact can be exponentially beneficial.


Finally we have perceived corruption, which needs to be kept in check to ensure a happier society.
